Oranges with red inside, like blood oranges, get their color from a pigment called anthocyanin, which develops during warm days and cooler nights. They have a unique sweet-tart taste, often with raspberry notes, and are rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and fiber. There are also varieties like the Cara Cara orange, which have pinkish-red flesh due to another pigment, lycopene.
